Customer Void Payment Request

Please follow the step below for processing customers application

Objective:
  • To validate if the payment was received.
  • To guide the customer on how to change modes of payment.

Assumptions:
  • The customer settled a Proforma and Recurring Invoice in the Customer portal.
  • The customer wants to validate if the payment was received.
  • The customer is interested in an alternative payment channel.

STEPS


Billing and Collection (Refund)

  1. B&C receives a ticket from CDMG-CS regarding billing concerns and evaluates the concern.
    1. If the Customer paid using the online and e-wallet payment option, check if the invoice is in “Pending status” for more than 60 minutes. If yes, the customer will receive a warning message. If not, proceed to Step 2. If they receive a warning and continue then proceed to Step 2. If they didn’t proceed, |Process ends here.
    2. If the customer paid using the Over The Counter payment option,check if the invoice is in “Pending status” for more than 48 hours. If yes, the customer will receive a warning message. If not, proceed to Step 2. If they receive a warning and continue then proceed to Step 2. If they didn’t proceed, |Process ends here.
  2. CDMG-CS checks if there have been any deductions from the customer’s account balance.
    1. If yes, ask the customer for a proof of payment
  • Receipt
  • Screenshot of successful transaction
  • Ref. No.
  • Email Address
If payment is valid, proceed to Step 3. If payment is invalid, Transaction status will remain "Pending" on Dragonpay System. Proceed to 2a. If not, proceed to Step 4.

IT

  1. If payment is valid, IT will contact Dragonpay to request for revalidation. Payment will be updated to success. |Process ends here.

Billing and Collection (Refund)

  1. B&C Proceed to void payment, B&C clicks the Void payment button. |Process ends here.. The "Pending" Invoice will be updated to "Unpaid" in the Customer portal and the customer may proceed on paying the same invoice in the Customer portal.
